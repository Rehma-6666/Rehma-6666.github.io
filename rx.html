About Me

I’m an interdisciplinary designer and researcher exploring the space between accessibility, storytelling, and creative technology. I’ve worked with the Helen Keller Center, NYU’s DigITs team at MSK, and others to build experiences that uncover overlooked narratives through design.

Projects

Helen Keller Center – VIP Research

Designed educational games (scavenger hunt & escape room) to develop critical thinking skills in blind teens. Presented concepts to HKC leadership as a foundation for their “Intiva” initiative.

UX Design Project – Helen Keller Center

Created accessible UX prototypes through a design class collaboration with HKC. Focused on usability testing and experience design for visually impaired youth.

Washington Square News – Photojournalism

Captured and edited visual stories for NYU’s student-run publication using Adobe Creative Suite and DSLR photography.

Future Fashion Zine – Writing

Published an article on sustainability in fashion, exploring how visual storytelling and research can drive awareness of environmental impact.
